Constitutional: Amparo as a Vital Guarantee

Category: Constitutional

In the context of corporate Criminal Law, constitutional amparo represents the final and most forceful line of defense against arbitrary or disproportionate State actions that threaten the operational continuity of a company or the freedom of its directors.

I. Amparo in the contemporary procedural environment

A company under investigation for alleged tax crimes sees all of its bank accounts blocked, including those intended for paying payroll and current tax obligations. The measure, issued without a prior hearing, threatens the continuity of the business and the rights of uninvestigated third parties. The amparo, requesting the partial lifting of the measure, allows for the restoration of proportionality.

Violation of the right to defense: obstruction of access to the file, restriction of contact with the defense attorney or unjustified refusal to practice evidence.

Violation of the natural judge guarantee: diversion of the knowledge of the matter to courts not competent by reason of the subject matter or territory.

II. Illustrative cases: when amparo becomes essential

Imagine the following hypothetical scenarios, representative of real situations faced by companies and directors:

Case I — Total blockage of operational accounts:

A company under investigation for alleged tax crimes sees all of its bank accounts blocked, including those intended for paying payroll and current tax obligations. The measure, issued without a prior hearing, threatens the continuity of the business and the rights of uninvestigated third parties. The amparo, requesting the partial lifting of the measure, allows for the restoration of proportionality.

Case II — Preventive deprivation without sufficient motivation:

A financial director is deprived of liberty under the imputation of corporate fraud, without the hearing minutes reflecting the elements of conviction that base the measure nor justifying the alleged flight risk. The filing of an amparo before the superior court manages to obtain the review and substitution of the measure for a less burdensome one.

Case III — Restriction of access to the file:

The defense of a compliance officer charged for omission of control in suspicious operations systematically requests access to the file, being denied repeatedly and without foundation. The amparo, invoking the violation of the right to defense and due process, forces the jurisdictional body to guarantee access.

Case IV — Inadequate jurisdiction:

A criminal case for alleged financial crimes is referred, without legal foundation, to a special court other than the one competent by reason of the subject matter. Given the inaction of ordinary appeals, constitutional amparo allows restoring the right to a natural judge, a first-order guarantee in any process.

III. Limits and risks of the use of amparo

A full understanding of amparo requires recognizing, with the same clarity with which its virtues are affirmed, its limits and the risks of its improper use. Amparo is not and cannot become a mechanism to evade the criminal process, weaken the legitimate investigative activity of the State or substitute ordinary appeals when these are suitable and effective.

  • Subsidiarity: Before resorting to it, the affected party must exhaust the remedies that the criminal process itself offers.
  • Strategy vs. tactics: A merely dilatory or tactical use of amparo not only exposes the applicant to its declaration of inadmissibility, but can also erode procedural credibility.
  • Rigorous diagnosis: It requires precisely identifying the fundamental right violated, verifying that there are no ordinary channels, and building a solid constitutional argument.

The responsible use of amparo requires that the criminal lawyer make a rigorous diagnosis of the situation. Strategic amparo —which protects genuinely threatened rights— is a high-impact instrument; dilatory amparo is a risky bet.

IV. Recommendations for companies and directors

The best defense is undoubtedly prevention. Companies with robust criminal compliance systems are in a position to early detect risk situations. Implement an effective criminal compliance program. Document all relevant corporate actions. Act early in the face of any procedural irregularity. Coordinate your criminal defense team with your compliance area. Evaluate amparo as a strategic tool, not as a desperate last resort. Establish internal response protocols to precautionary measures.

V. Amparo as a guarantee of the Rule of Law

Constitutional amparo is not an anomaly of the system; it is its most refined expression. Its existence as an autonomous, expeditious, and constitutional-ranking action reflects the conviction that no process —however legitimate its purposes may be— can become an instrument of injury to the rights recognized by the Constitution.

VI. Conclusion

In the contemporary procedural environment, amparo fulfills an essential counterweight function. It does not defend impunity; it defends the legality of the path that leads to the truth and eventual criminal liability. And that function, far from being an obstacle to justice, is a condition of its legitimacy.

VII. Specialized Advisory

For those working in the corporate world —owners, directors, accountants, compliance officers— understanding amparo is not an academic question: it is a first-order legal risk management tool that can, at the critical moment, preserve freedom, reputation, and business continuity.
